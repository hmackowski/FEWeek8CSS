What are Pokémon?

Pokémon, short for "Pocket Monsters," are creatures that inhabit the Pokémon world. They were first introduced in 1996 by Nintendo, Game Freak, and Creatures as a video game franchise. Since then, Pokémon has expanded into various forms of media, including trading card games, an animated TV series, movies, and more.


Characteristics of Pokémon

  • Species: Each Pokémon belongs to a specific species, such as Pikachu, Bulbasaur, or Charizard. There are currently over 1000 different species of Pokémon.

  • Types: Pokémon have different elemental types, such as Fire, Water, Grass, Electric, Psychic, and more. These types determine their strengths and weaknesses in battles.

  • Abilities: Each Pokémon possesses unique abilities and skills. These abilities can range from special attacks and defenses to utility moves and healing powers.

  • Evolution: Many Pokémon have multiple evolutionary stages. They start as basic forms and can evolve into more powerful forms as they gain experience or fulfill certain conditions.

  • Trainers: In the Pokémon world, trainers are individuals who capture and train Pokémon. Trainers form a bond with their Pokémon and battle against other trainers to prove their skills.
